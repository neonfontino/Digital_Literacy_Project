# Social Media Responsibility Checklist for College Students

To maintain a professional and safe digital presence as a Student Digital Ambassador, it is essential to follow these guidelines for responsible social media use. Following these simple steps ensures that your online footprint remains an asset rather than a liability during your academic and professional journey.

### The Do’s (Best Practices)
* **Update Security Settings:** Regularly audit your privacy settings and ensure Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) is active on all platforms.
* **Build a Professional Brand:** Use platforms like LinkedIn to showcase your projects, certifications, and academic achievements to potential recruiters.
* **Fact-Check Before Sharing:** Always verify the authenticity of news or college-related notices before hitting the share button to prevent the spread of misinformation.
* **Engage Positively:** Use your digital voice to support peers, share helpful resources, and participate in constructive academic discussions.
* **Curate Your Tagged Content:** Regularly review photos or posts you are tagged in to ensure they align with the professional image you want to project.

### The Don’ts (Risks to Avoid)
* **Don't Post Sensitive Info:** Never share photos of your college ID, hall tickets, or real-time locations that could compromise your physical or digital safety.
* **Don't Engage in Cyberbullying:** Avoid participating in toxic online arguments, "flame wars," or posting derogatory comments about faculty and fellow students.
* **Don't Overshare Personal Struggles:** While being authentic is fine, avoid using professional or public platforms to post unprofessional rants or highly private details.
* **Don't Plagiarize Content:** Never post someone else's digital work, code, or designs as your own without providing proper credit or citations.
* **Don't Ignore Your Digital Footprint:** Don't assume that "deleted" posts are gone forever; always post with the mindset that an employer might see it in the future.
