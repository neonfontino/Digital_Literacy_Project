# Social Media Responsibility Checklist for College Students  

Using social media is normal, but as a Student Digital Ambassador, it’s not just casual anymore.  
What you post (or even what you’re tagged in) can affect how people see you later—especially in academic or professional settings.  

Not everything needs to be perfect, but a few basic habits help avoid unnecessary problems.

---

### The Do’s (Best Practices)

- **Check your privacy + security settings sometimes**  
  Most people set it once and forget it.  
  But it’s worth checking again—2FA, visibility, account settings, etc. Just to be safe.

- **Actually use LinkedIn (not just create it)**  
  Having a profile is one thing, but updating it is different.  
  Even small things like adding a project or certificate make a difference over time.

- **Pause before sharing anything**  
  Especially college news or “important” updates.  
  A lot of things circulate without being verified, and once it’s shared, it spreads quickly.

- **Keep interactions normal / respectful**  
  Not everything needs a reply.  
  But if you do respond, tone matters more than expected—especially in public discussions.

- **Look at tagged posts once in a while**  
  This is easy to ignore.  
  But sometimes you’re tagged in things that don’t really match how you want your profile to look.

---

### The Don’ts (Risks to Avoid)

- **Don’t post sensitive stuff**  
  Like ID cards, hall tickets, or even live locations.  
  It might seem harmless at the time, but it’s not really safe.

- **Avoid getting pulled into arguments**  
  Especially online ones that just keep going.  
  Most of the time, nothing comes out of it except unnecessary attention.

- **Try not to overshare personal things**  
  Being open is fine, but not everything needs to be posted publicly.  
  Some things are better kept private (depends on the situation, but still).

- **Don’t copy content without credit**  
  Code, designs, posts—anything.  
  Even if it’s small, it’s better to mention the source than ignore it.

- **Don’t assume deleting = gone**  
  Once something is online, it can still exist somewhere.  
  Screenshots, backups… you never really know.  

---

None of these points are complicated on their own.  
But usually it’s small things (like not checking once, or posting without thinking) that lead to bigger issues later.
