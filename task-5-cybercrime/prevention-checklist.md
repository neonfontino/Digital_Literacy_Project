# "Stay Safe Online" – Prevention Checklist  

As a Student Digital Ambassador, these are some basic things I noted down that actually help in avoiding common scams and issues online:

---

1. **Check the URL properly**  
   Don’t just open any link and log in.  
   Official websites (like VIT or banks) won’t use random short links like bit.ly for login pages. If the URL looks odd, it probably is.

2. **Use MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)**  
   It’s a bit annoying sometimes, but it helps.  
   Enabling MFA on email, LinkedIn, banking apps, etc., adds an extra layer so even if someone gets your password, they still can’t access everything easily.

3. **Look at the sender carefully**  
   Not just the name—the actual email ID.  
   Sometimes it looks real at first, but small spelling changes give it away (like “vlt” instead of “vit”). Easy to miss if you’re not paying attention.

4. **UPI – Important rule about PIN**  
   You never need to enter your PIN to *receive* money.  
   If someone says you need to enter it for a refund or anything similar, that’s a scam. No exceptions.

5. **QR codes aren’t always safe**  
   Especially ones sent on WhatsApp or social media.  
   If it’s from an unknown person promising rewards/prizes, it’s better not to scan at all.

6. **Avoid public Wi-Fi for important logins**  
   Using café or station Wi-Fi for browsing is fine, but not for logging into bank accounts or academic portals.  
   It’s just not secure enough.

7. **Keep devices updated**  
   Updates feel unnecessary sometimes, but they actually fix security issues.  
   Ignoring them for too long can leave your device exposed.

8. **Know where to report problems**  
   If something feels like a scam or already happened, don’t ignore it. Reporting early can actually help.

---

### Reporting (India)

- National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in  
- Helpline: 1930 (works 24x7)

---

Not everything here is complicated, but missing even one small detail is usually how people get caught in scams.
